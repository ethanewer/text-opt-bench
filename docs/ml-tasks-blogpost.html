text-opt-bm

Benchmarking agents on harder ML systems tasks

Three locally scored tasks ask an optimization agent to improve an LLM router, synthesize an optimizer that transfers across loss landscapes, and compress a small language model under a physical storage constraint. This page reports the first complete three-run trial.

  • suite LLM routing, optimizer generalization, and SLM weight compression.
  • budget Three independent one-hour optimization runs per task.
  • agent gpt-5.6-sol with high reasoning.
  • local CPU-first scoring; Apple MPS for model compression.

For compression, BPW measures the complete submitted bundle, not a nominal weight-bit label. Every plotted compression baseline is a locally decoded and graded checkpoint below the 3.5-BPW limit. GPTQ and HQQ use a same-bit RTN tail for remaining LFM tensors. The AQLM line uses three additive 8-bit codebooks over 8-weight vectors with activation-Hessian optimization and calibration-scale point subsampling.

What the first trial says. Routing shows a modest, repeatable improvement over recognizable local baselines. Compression shows a much larger improvement and produces three independently feasible checkpoints, with the best checkpoint remaining stable across ID and OOD data. Optimizer synthesis also improves strongly, but the gap between known and unseen test families—and the absence of stronger learned-optimizer baselines—makes it the task most in need of further validation before treating its current frontier as a research result.
Experiment 1b was measured with gpt-5.6-sol at high reasoning, three independent one-hour runs per task. Scores shown here were captured on July 12, 2026. All test values belong to validation-selected incumbents; lower is better. Queued evaluation time is excluded from the x-axis. text-opt-bm is a work in progress.
